The Catalog is where you define everything your salon offers — the services your team performs, the products you sell, membership packages, gift vouchers, and your product inventory. Think of it as the foundation that powers your bookings and checkout.

Click **Catalog** in the left menu to get started. You'll see five tabs at the top: **Services**, **Products**, **Memberships**, **Vouchers**, and **Stock Audit**.

---

## Services

![Catalog Services](/catalog/14_catalog_service.gif)

Services are the treatments your team performs — haircuts, color, manicures, massages, and so on. Every service you add here will be available to select when booking an appointment.

### Adding a service

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Catalog → Services** and click **Add Service**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Give the service a **name**, choose a **category** (e.g., Hair, Nails), set the **duration** in minutes, and enter the **price**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Make sure the status is set to **Active** — only active services show up when booking.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Editing or removing a service

Click the **Edit** icon on any service to update its details. If you want to stop a service from showing up in bookings without deleting it permanently, just toggle the status to inactive. To remove it entirely, use the **Delete** option.

## Products

![Catalog Products](/catalog/15_catalog_products.gif)

Products are the retail or consumable items you sell or use in your salon — shampoos, styling products, skincare, and so on. NxCut tracks your stock levels automatically as products are sold.

### Adding a product

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Catalog → Products** and click **Add Product**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Enter the product's **name**, **brand**, **category**, and **supplier**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Set the **price** and enter your current **stock quantity**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Toggle it to **Active** so it's available to add during checkout.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Every time a product is sold at checkout, your stock count goes down automatically. Use the [Stock Audit](#stock-audit) tab when you want to check your physical stock against what the system shows.

## Memberships

![Catalog Memberships](/catalog/17_catalog_membership.gif)

Memberships are prepaid session packages — a client pays upfront for a bundle of appointments (for example, 10 haircuts for the price of 8). Each time they come in and use a session, it's deducted from their balance automatically.

### Creating a membership

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Catalog → Memberships** and click **Add Membership**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Give the membership a **name** and choose which **service** it applies to.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Set the number of **sessions** included and the total **price**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Optionally set an **expiry period** — for example, all sessions must be used within 12 months of purchase.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once a client buys a membership at checkout, their session count is tracked automatically on their [client profile](/docs/clients). Sessions are used up each time they check out using that membership.

## Vouchers

![Catalog Vouchers](/catalog/18_catalog_voucher.gif)

Vouchers are gift cards or discount codes that clients can redeem when paying for services or products. You set the value (either a fixed amount or a percentage off), and NxCut tracks how much has been used and what's left.

### Creating a voucher

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Catalog → Vouchers** and click **Add Voucher**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Give the voucher a **name** and choose the discount type: a **fixed amount** (e.g., AED 100 off) or a **percentage** (e.g., 20% off).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Create a **voucher code** — this is what the client enters at checkout.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Optionally set an **expiry date** and a **usage limit** (e.g., can only be used once).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
You can check a client's remaining voucher balance and full usage history on their profile under [Clients → Voucher Activity](/docs/clients#voucher-activity).
</Callout>

## Stock Audit

A stock audit is how you check that your physical inventory matches what NxCut thinks you have. It's good practice to run one regularly, especially if products are used during services as well as sold.

A stock audit moves through three stages: **Stock Session → Stock Entry → Stock Review**. Each stage has its own status so you always know where an audit is up to.

| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| In Progress | The audit is open and items are being counted |
| Review | Counting is done — ready to review and confirm |
| Completed | The audit is finalised and can no longer be edited |

---

### Step 1 — Stock Session

![Catalog Stock Audit Session](/catalog/16_1_catalog_stock_audit_session.gif)

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Go to **Catalog → Stock Audit** and click **Start New Audit Session**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Select the products you want to include in this count.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Start** to begin. The audit moves to the **Stock Entry** stage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

### Step 2 — Stock Entry (Counting)
![Catalog Stock Audit Entry](/catalog/16_2_catalog_stock_audit_entry.gif)

This is where you physically count your stock. The entry screen opens in **barcode mode** by default — just scan a product's barcode and NxCut will find it automatically and add 1 to its counted quantity. Each scan is saved instantly, so you don't need to do anything extra.

<Callout type="tip">
Scan the same product multiple times to keep adding to its count — each scan increments the quantity by 1.
</Callout>

**If you click Save Stock Audit before finishing:**

A dialog will appear telling you how many products still haven't been counted (e.g., "5 products remaining"). The only option here is **Continue** — this sends you back to finish counting before the audit can be saved.

**If you click the X (close) button before finishing:**

A dialog titled **"Uncounted products remaining"** will appear, asking how you'd like to handle the items you didn't get to. You'll have two choices:

- **Save uncounted as 0** — records those products as having zero stock on the shelf. Use this if you genuinely checked and found nothing.
- **Sync counted to previous stock** — leaves those products at whatever stock value was already in the system. Use this if you simply didn't get around to counting them and don't want to affect their records.

<Callout type="info">
Not sure which to pick? If you didn't actually check those products, choose **Sync counted to previous stock** — that way only the items you physically counted will be updated.
</Callout>

---

### Step 3 — Stock Review
![Catalog Stock Audit Review](/catalog/16_3_catalog_stock_audit_review.gif)

Once all items are counted and saved, the audit moves to **Review** status. On this page you'll see:

- An overview of every product that was counted
- The **quantity difference** between what the system expected and what you physically counted
- The **price difference** — the value impact of any discrepancies

From here you can:
- **Resume editing** — go back and adjust any counts before finalising
- **Complete the audit** — confirm the counts are correct and lock the audit

<Callout type="warning">
Once you click **Complete**, the audit is finalised and cannot be edited. Make sure everything looks right before confirming.
</Callout>
